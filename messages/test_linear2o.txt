Notes:

    If the eigenvalues are distinct and real, and unequal in absolute
    value, then the ratio of consecutive terms of most of the sequences
    will approximate the the eigenvalue which is larger in absolute value.
    In some situations, we can pair the sequences, as we did for the
    Fibonacci sequences and the root 2 sequences.

    If the coefficients in the Binet formula are A and B corresponding to
    eigenvalues a and b, then for all integers n:

        s[n+2] = αs[n+1] + βs[n]            # shift equation
        s[0] = s₀                           # initial condition
        s[1] = s₁                           # initial condition
        s[n] = Aaⁿ + Bbⁿ                    # Binet formula

    In some cases, we obtain "best rational estimates", but this is not, in
    general, true.  (The mixed estimates obtained for the golden ratio and
    one plus the square root of two are best rational estimates -- they are
    precisely the estimates obtained using the continued fraction algorithm.)
